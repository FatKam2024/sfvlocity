Customization Fundamentals in Salesforce Vlocity OM

Learn the essential techniques for customizing Salesforce Vlocity Order Management to meet your organization's unique requirements.

Introduction

Customization is key to tailoring Salesforce Vlocity OM to your specific business needs. This guide will introduce you to the fundamental customization techniques available in the platform.

Key Customization Areas

  • Custom Fields: Creating additional fields to capture unique data points.
  • Page Layouts: Modifying the layout of record pages to optimize user experience.
  • Custom Objects: Developing new objects to represent unique business entities.
  • Validation Rules: Implementing data validation to ensure data integrity.
  • Workflow Rules: Automating business processes based on specific criteria.

Hands-On Lab: Basic OM Customization

This lab will guide you through the process of performing basic customization tasks in Salesforce Vlocity OM.

Lab Steps

  1. Log into your Salesforce org with Vlocity OM installed.
  2. Create a custom field on the Order object to track a unique business requirement.
  3. Modify the Order page layout to include your new custom field.
  4. Create a simple validation rule to ensure the custom field is populated when required.
  5. Set up a workflow rule to automatically update a field based on certain criteria.
  6. Test your customizations by creating and editing sample orders.

Best Practices for OM Customization

  • Always consider the impact of customizations on system performance and user experience.
  • Use declarative tools (point-and-click) whenever possible before resorting to code-based solutions.
  • Maintain clear documentation of all customizations for easier maintenance and upgrades.
  • Regularly review and optimize your customizations to ensure they continue to meet business needs.
  • Leverage Salesforce sandboxes to test customizations before deploying to production.

Advanced Customization Topics

  • Apex Triggers: Writing custom code to handle complex business logic.
  • Visualforce Pages: Creating custom user interfaces for specific requirements.
  • Lightning Components: Developing reusable UI components for Lightning Experience.
  • Custom APIs: Building custom APIs to integrate with external systems.
