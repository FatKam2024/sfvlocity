OM Glossary

This glossary provides definitions for common terms used in Salesforce Vlocity Order Management (OM).

Order
A request from a customer to purchase products or services.
Order Capture
The process of collecting and recording order information from various channels.
Order Processing
The series of steps involved in handling an order from its initial capture to fulfillment.
Order Fulfillment
The process of delivering the products or services requested in an order to the customer.
Order Lifecycle
The entire journey of an order from creation to completion, including all intermediate stages.
Order Status
The current state of an order in its lifecycle, such as "New," "In Process," or "Completed."
Order Item
An individual product or service line within an order.
Workflow Rule
An automated process that triggers specific actions based on predefined criteria in Salesforce.
Process Builder
A Salesforce tool for creating automated processes with a visual interface.
Omniscript
A Vlocity tool for creating guided, interactive processes for tasks like order capture or configuration.
Order Management Console
A centralized interface for managing and processing orders in Vlocity OM.
Order Routing
The process of directing orders to the appropriate fulfillment channels or teams based on specific criteria.
Backorder
An order that cannot be fulfilled immediately due to lack of inventory.
Split Shipment
When items from a single order are shipped separately, often due to availability or logistics reasons.
Order Orchestration
The coordination and management of complex order processes across multiple systems or departments.
Exception Handling
The process of managing orders that deviate from the standard fulfillment process due to errors or special requirements.
Order Validation
The process of checking an order for accuracy and completeness before processing.
SLA (Service Level Agreement)
A commitment to fulfill orders within a specified timeframe.
Order Tracking
The ability to monitor and report on the status and location of an order throughout its lifecycle.
Returns Management
The process of handling customer returns, including authorizations, refunds, and restocking.
