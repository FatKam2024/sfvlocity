CPQ Glossary

This glossary provides definitions for common terms used in Salesforce Vlocity Configure, Price, Quote (CPQ).

CPQ
An acronym for Configure, Price, Quote, a process that streamlines the sales process by automating product configuration, pricing, and quote generation.
Product Bundle
A collection of products and/or services that are sold together as a single unit.
Price Book
A list of products and their associated prices that can be used when creating quotes in CPQ.
Quote
A formal document that outlines the products or services a customer wishes to purchase, along with the pricing details.
Discount Schedule
A set of rules that define how discounts are applied to products or services based on various criteria.
Approval Workflow
An automated process that requires certain quotes to be reviewed and approved by specific roles before they can be finalized.
Configuration
The process of selecting and combining products or services to meet the customer’s needs within CPQ.
Guided Selling
A feature that helps sales representatives through the product selection and configuration process by providing recommendations and automating steps based on the customer’s requirements.
Dynamic Pricing
Pricing that can change based on various factors such as demand, customer segments, or competitive pricing.
Contract
A formal agreement between a buyer and seller that outlines the terms of the purchase, including the products, services, prices, and conditions.
Subscription
A model of selling products or services where the customer pays on a recurring basis, such as monthly or annually.
Amendment
An adjustment or change to an existing contract or quote, often involving changes to the products, pricing, or terms.
Renewal
The process of extending a subscription or contract for another term, often with the option to adjust the terms or pricing.
Quote Template
A predefined format used to generate quotes, ensuring consistency in presentation and information.
Margin
The difference between the cost of a product or service and its selling price, often expressed as a percentage.
Upsell
The practice of encouraging customers to purchase a more expensive or premium version of a product or service they are considering.
Cross-sell
The practice of selling additional products or services to a customer that complement the items they are purchasing.
