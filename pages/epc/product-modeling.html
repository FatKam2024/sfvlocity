Product Modeling

In this section, we explore how to model products in Salesforce Vlocity's EPC, covering the key concepts, attributes, and relationships. Effective product modeling is crucial for creating a flexible and scalable product catalog that can support complex offerings and bundles.

Understanding Product Models

Product models define the structure and components of a product, allowing for customization and scalability. Each model can include multiple attributes, such as size, color, and material, and can be linked to other models through relationships.

Key Components of Product Modeling

  • Attributes: Define the characteristics of the product. These can be technical specifications, pricing information, or any other relevant details.
  • Relationships: Establish connections between different product components. This includes parent-child relationships, dependencies, and compatibilities.
  • Versioning: Manage different versions of a product model to track changes over time and maintain historical data.
  • Rules and Constraints: Define business logic that governs how products can be configured or sold.
  • Lifecycle Management: Control the availability and visibility of products based on their lifecycle stage.

Lab: Creating a Product Model

Follow these steps to create a basic product model in Salesforce Vlocity EPC:

  1. Navigate to the EPC module in Salesforce.
  2. Click on "New" to create a new product model.
  3. Define the basic information:
    • Name: Enter a descriptive name for your product
    • Code: Assign a unique identifier
    • Description: Provide a detailed description of the product
  4. Add attributes to your product model:
    • Click on "New Attribute"
    • Define attribute name, data type, and any default values
    • Specify if the attribute is required or optional
  5. Create relationships with other product models:
    • Navigate to the "Relationships" tab
    • Click "New Relationship" and select the related product model
    • Define the type of relationship (e.g., parent-child, cross-sell)
  6. Set up rules and constraints:
    • Go to the "Rules" section
    • Create validation rules or compatibility rules as needed
  7. Test the product model:
    • Use the "Preview" function to simulate how the product will appear in CPQ
    • Verify that all attributes, relationships, and rules work as expected

Advanced Topics

Explore advanced product modeling techniques to create more sophisticated and dynamic product offerings:

  • Dynamic Models: Create product models that adjust based on customer input or market conditions. For example, a telecommunications package that changes its offerings based on the customer's location or usage patterns.
  • Nested Configurations: Model complex products with multiple levels of configuration, such as a customizable computer system with various component options.
  • Product Bundles: Learn to create and manage product bundles that combine multiple individual products into a single offering, including pricing and discount strategies for bundles.
  • Attribute-based Configuration: Implement advanced attribute-based configuration to allow for highly customizable products while maintaining data integrity and system performance.
  • Integration with External Systems: Explore techniques for integrating product models with external systems, such as inventory management or supply chain systems, to ensure real-time accuracy of product information.

Best Practices

  • Start with a clear understanding of your product hierarchy and relationships before modeling.
  • Use consistent naming conventions for attributes and relationships across all product models.
  • Regularly review and optimize your product models to ensure they align with current business needs.
  • Leverage versioning to manage product changes over time without disrupting existing orders or quotes.
  • Document your product modeling decisions and rationale to facilitate knowledge sharing and future maintenance.

By mastering these product modeling techniques, you'll be able to create a flexible and powerful product catalog that can adapt to changing business needs and support complex sales scenarios.
