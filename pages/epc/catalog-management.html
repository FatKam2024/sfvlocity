Catalog Management

Catalog management in EPC allows you to organize products, offers, and pricing into logical groups that align with your business strategy. Effective catalog management is crucial for presenting the right products to the right customers at the right time.

Creating and Managing Catalogs

Learn how to create catalogs in Salesforce Vlocity, including organizing products and setting up rules for catalog visibility and access.

Steps to Create a Catalog

  1. Navigate to the EPC module in Salesforce Vlocity.
  2. Click on "New Catalog" to start the creation process.
  3. Define the catalog details:
    • Name: Choose a descriptive name for your catalog
    • Code: Assign a unique identifier
    • Description: Provide a detailed description of the catalog's purpose
    • Status: Set the initial status (e.g., Draft, Active, Inactive)
  4. Add products and offers to the catalog:
    • Use the "Add Products" function to select products from your product models
    • Organize products into categories or subcatalogs if needed
    • Set product sequence for display order
  5. Set up rules for catalog visibility and access:
    • Define user roles or profiles that can access the catalog
    • Set up account-based or segment-based visibility rules
    • Configure time-based availability (e.g., seasonal catalogs)
  6. Apply pricing and discounting rules specific to the catalog if needed.
  7. Set up any catalog-specific attributes or fields.
  8. Test the catalog setup in a sandbox environment:
    • Verify product visibility and ordering
    • Test access rules with different user profiles
    • Ensure pricing and discounts are applied correctly
  9. Activate the catalog once testing is complete.

Advanced Catalog Management Techniques

Explore advanced catalog management techniques to create more sophisticated and dynamic catalogs:

  • Dynamic Catalogs: Create catalogs that adjust based on customer segmentation, market trends, or other variables. For example, a catalog that shows different product offerings based on a customer's industry or size.
  • Multi-Language Catalogs: Set up catalogs to support multiple languages for global businesses, ensuring consistent product information across different regions.
  • Personalized Catalogs: Implement rules to personalize catalog contents based on customer attributes, purchase history, or behavior.
  • Channel-Specific Catalogs: Manage different catalogs for various sales channels (e.g., direct sales, partner sales, e-commerce) with channel-specific pricing and product offerings.
  • Time-Based Catalog Management: Implement catalogs that automatically update based on time factors, such as promotional periods or product lifecycle stages.
  • Approval Workflows: Set up approval processes for catalog changes to ensure proper review and compliance before updates go live.

Best Practices for Catalog Management

  • Maintain a clear and consistent structure across all catalogs to improve user experience and manageability.
  • Regularly review and optimize your catalogs to ensure they align with current business strategies and market demands.
  • Use version control for catalogs to track changes over time and enable easy rollback if needed.
  • Implement a governance process for catalog updates to maintain data integrity and consistency.
  • Leverage analytics to understand catalog performance and make data-driven decisions on product offerings and pricing.
  • Ensure proper integration between your catalogs and other systems (e.g., ERP, CRM) for consistent product information across the organization.
  • Provide thorough training to sales and customer service teams on how to effectively use and navigate the catalogs.
